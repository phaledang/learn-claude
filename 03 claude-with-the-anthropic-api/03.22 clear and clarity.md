### 📌 Key Concepts

* **The first line of your prompt is critical**
  * It sets the context and strongly influences output quality.
* Focus on **2 principles**:
  * **Clarity**
  * **Directness**

***

### ✅ Clear Communication

* Use **simple, unambiguous language**
* State **exactly what you want**
* Start with a **clear task statement**

**Example**

* ❌ Vague:  
  “I need to know about those things people put on their roofs that use sun…”
* ✅ Clear:  
  “Write three paragraphs about how solar panels work.”

***

### ✅ Direct Instructions

* Use **instructions, not questions**
* Start with **action verbs**
  * e.g., *Write, Generate, Create, Identify*

**Example**

* ❌ Question style:  
  “What countries use geothermal energy?”
* ✅ Direct instruction:  
  “Identify three countries that use geothermal energy. Include generation stats.”

***

### ✅ Improved Prompt Structure

Transform weak prompts → strong prompts:

* ❌ Weak:  
  “What should this person eat?”
* ✅ Strong:  
  “Generate a one-day meal plan for an athlete that meets their dietary restrictions.”

👉 Benefits:

* Clearly defines:
  * Action (Generate)
  * Output (Meal plan)
  * Constraints (1 day, athlete, dietary restrictions)

***

### 📈 Impact

* Better prompt structure → **better model performance**
* Example improvement:
  * Score increased from **2.32 → 3.92** just by rewriting the prompt

***

## ❓ Q\&A

**Q1. Why is the first line of a prompt important?**  
A: It sets expectations and determines how well the model understands the task.

**Q2. What does “clear” mean in prompting?**  
A: Using simple, precise language with no ambiguity.

**Q3. What does “direct” mean in prompting?**  
A: Giving explicit instructions using action verbs instead of asking questions.

**Q4. Give examples of good action verbs.**  
A: Write, Generate, Create, Identify.

**Q5. What improvements come from better prompts?**  
A: More accurate, relevant, and structured responses.
